April 2-5, 2006. Venice Convention at The Cinema Palace, Venice Lido - Italy
Call for Posters
 
 
Poster submission is now open.
Submission deadline: January 20.

Posters reporting on original research (both theoretical and experimental) in all areas of computational molecular biology are currently being sought. Examples are:
  • Genomics
  • Molecular sequence analysis
  • Recognition of genes and regulatory elements
  • Molecular evolution
  • Protein structure
  • Gene expression
  • Gene networks
  • Combinatorial libraries and drug design
  • Computational proteomics
  • Computational genomics
  • Microarray design and data analysis
  • Structural and functional genomics
The following instructions must be followed exactly if a poster is to be accepted. Acceptance is conditional on at least one of its authors pre-registering for RECOMB electronically by Feb 3, 2006.
Poster abstracts should be submitted online in a pdf format. Templates are available for common text editors: LaTeX, Word. Please download the template of your choice, and edit it with the appropriate software program. Make sure that during editing you preserve all formating parameters of the original template (for example, do not remove any formatting tags in the LaTeX template). The length of the abstract should fit on two pages after conversion to pdf. Files we cannot open or which do not satisfy the format instructions will be returned.

In summary
  • All abstracts must be submitted via our web site by the abstract deadline 11:59pm, (local time) January 20, 2006.
  • During the submission process, check that the information submitted in different form fields (abstract title, authors, etc) is in agreement with the content of the abstract.
  • At least one of the poster authors must be present at the conference.
  • Space for posters is limited, and we may not be able to accept all poster submissions. If the amount of poster abstracts exceeds the capacity, every effort will be made to ensure that all groups who wish to participate in the poster are represented.
  • Presenters of accepted posters will be notified by February 3, 2006.
  • The size of the poster space is: Height: 1m, Width: 1m. Fasteners will be provided at the site, please DO NOT bring tape, tacks or pins.
  • There is a maximum of two posters per presenting author.
